Product spec · Clinic management platform

Information architecture

Four apps · screen hierarchy · navigation flows · cross-app handoffs

The map before the mockups. What screens exist in each app, how users move between them, and how the four apps connect into one product.
How to read this

The product is four apps sharing one data backbone. Front Desk is for reception and billing. Doc App is the doctor's clinical workspace. Diagnostic App is for the lab and imaging team. Patient App is for the patient on mobile.

This document covers four things in order: a system map showing what the four apps are and how they connect; the shared services they all consume; cross-app handoffs end-to-end; and then each app's screen hierarchy and internal navigation flows. Mockups follow in a separate document.

§3

Cross-app handoffs

Where one app's action triggers state change in another. These are the seams that have to be designed deliberately — silent failures here cause user confusion across apps.

PatPatient books appointment→ Front Desk queue + Doc App schedule + scheduling servicePre-consult symptom form filled by patient surfaces in Doc App before patient arrives
FDPatient marked "arrived"→ Doc App today's queue · token assigned · live position broadcast to Patient AppTriage urgency is set here; emergency / pediatric / elderly jump the queue
DocDoctor orders test→ Diagnostic App order queue · pricing pulled into Front Desk cartReflex rules may auto-add tests; allergy / drug checks fire on prescription
DocPrescription finalised→ Patient App (with plain-language note) · in-house pharmacy queue · Front Desk cartRefill rules captured at original prescribing; allergy banner persists across visits
DiagSample collected→ Doc App ("sample drawn"); status visible in Patient App live screenTwo-factor patient ID verification at draw blocks mismatched samples
DiagResult verified & released→ Doc App (inline in encounter or later) · Patient App report viewer · WhatsApp + SMS notificationCritical values trigger an out-of-band escalation chain to the ordering doctor
FDBill paid→ Patient App receipt · accounting ledger · Tally export queue · diagnostic order activates if blocked on paymentVAT-exempt vs taxable lines kept distinct for IRD audit
PatPatient uploads external report→ Diagnostic App review queue · once tagged, surfaces in Doc App trend graphsRemoves the social friction of "telling on yourself" — explicitly welcomed
DocRecall scheduled→ rules engine · Patient App reminder · Front Desk recall list · re-engagement loopCondition-driven (HbA1c last > 90 days for diabetics, etc.) — not calendar-driven
DocCritical-value alert acknowledged→ escalation chain stops · Diagnostic App audit log records ack · patient notified per protocolIf unacked in N minutes, escalates: ordering → on-call → senior → owner
